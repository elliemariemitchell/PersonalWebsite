I am currently a Postgraduate Research Associate at Dartmouth College, where I work with Dr. Sean Westwood and the Polarization Research Lab. I received my Bachelor of Arts in Political Science from Brigham Young University in 2025. At BYU, I conducted research with Drs. Adam Brown, Christopher Karpowitz, and Ethan Busby, focusing on topics ranging from legislative professionalism and gendered cosponsorship to AI-driven political persuasion and voter behavior.

My research interests lie at the intersection of personal identity, legislative behavior, and political representation. I am particularly interested in how legislators’ identities, especially gender, influence deliberation, coalition-building, and constituent responsiveness across state legislatures, where variations in size and institutional rules create a rich setting to study these dynamics. Building on my prior research, I aim to construct large datasets from state legislatures nationwide and apply statistical analysis to understand how identities shape which ideas become law and which constituencies benefit most from policy outcomes.

My work has been published in State Politics and Policy Quarterly and I have presented my research at conferences including at the Midwest Political Science Association’s annual meeting and at BYU’s Mary Lou Fulton Mentored Learning Conference.
